About

I am an independent researcher on intelligence. My long-term research goal is to create an artificial researcher. I am currently intereseted in meta-learning, grounding, and memory.

I completed my master of research at the Department of Complexity Science and Engineering at the University of Tokyo. Prior to that, I received my B.A. in Economics at Keio University, where I studied economics, international relations, and neuroaesthetics.

Research

Statistical Mechanical Analysis of Online Learning

I used a statistical mechanical method to analyze the learning dynamics of shallow neural networks. My research employed the techniques of previous works by statistical physicians in the 1990s, in particular [Saad & Solla 1995] . With some assumptions, this framework allows us to derive the exact learning dynamics of two-layered neural networks. This technique is recently revived by a series of researches [Goldt et al. NeurIPS 2019] [Yoshida & Okada NeurIPS 2019] [Goldt et al. 2019] . I used this method to examine the impact of the normalization method on the learning dynamics.

Meta-learning

I studied the trainability of Model-agnostic Meta-learning (MAML), a popular meta-learning algorithm. With some simplifications, I regarded MAML as optimization with a negative gradient penalty. This simplification enables to capture of the empirical relationship between critical meta-learning rate and inner-learning rate. A takeaway from this study is the usefulness to regard gradient-based bi-level optimization algorithms as a single-level regularized optimization problem. This view could work to study other gradient-based bi-level optimization algorithms as well. I believe bi-level optimization matters because the problems we are interested in are usually hierarchical and bi-level optimization is a natural fit for the problems.
