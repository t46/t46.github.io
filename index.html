About

I received my B.A. in Economics at Keio University, where I studied international relations surpervised by Kanji Akagi and neuroaesthetics in the Aesthetic Science Laboratory advised by Hideaki Kawabata.

Research

I'm interested in theory of neural networks, influence of task and data structure on learning, and research automation.

Statistical Mechanical Analysis of Online Learning

We use statistical mechanics to analyze the learning dynamics of neural networks. My research depends on previous works by statistical physicians in 1990s, in particular [Saad & Solla 1995]. Assuming that input data are i.i.d. sample and that same data will not be used twice, this framework allows us to derive the exact learning dynamics of two-layered neural network in the wide limit of input width. We used this method to examin the impact of normalization method, i.e. batch normalization, on the learning dynamics of shallow networks.
