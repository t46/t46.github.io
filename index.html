About

I am an independent researcher on intelligence. My long-term research goal is to create an artificial researcher. I am interested in symbolic fluency, memory, and autonomy.

I completed my master of research at the Department of Complexity Science and Engineering at the University of Tokyo. Prior to that, I received my B.A. in Economics at Keio University, where I studied economics, international relations, and neuroaesthetics.

Research

I'm currently interested in how connectionist models can segment the world into symbol-like discrete primitives, form the symbolic system, and use the symbols to behave and think like humans. One of my ongoing projects is about systematic/compositional generalization. In the past I have been involved in research on the following topics:
  • NNGP (Neural Network Gaussian Process)
  • Topological data analysis
  • Continual learning
  • Meta-learning
  • Out-of-distribution genralization
  • Trainability of neural networks
  • Disentangled representation
  • Statistical mechanical analysis of learning dynamics of neural networks
  • Sense of agency
